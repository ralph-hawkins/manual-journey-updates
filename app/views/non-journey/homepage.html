Notify the Office for Product Safety and Standards (OPSS) about cosmetic products available to consumers in the UK.

What you need to do

You need to notify OPSS about every cosmetic product available to consumers in the UK.

! Warning You may face a fine of up to £5,000, or a prison term of up to 3 months, if you do not notify OPSS about a cosmetics product.

What you'll need to tell us

You’ll need to tell OPSS:

  • what's in the cosmetic product
  • the name and details of a contact person for the National Poisons Information Service, or Trading Standards, to get in touch with
  • who the ‘responsible person’ is
What's a ‘responsible person’?

The ‘responsible person’ (RP) is the individual or company responsible for ensuring the regulations are followed. Every cosmetic product placed on the UK market must have an RP. The RP must make sure:

  • cosmetic products placed on the market are safe for human health
  • every cosmetic product complies with Regulation 1223/2009

Nanomaterials

If the cosmetic product contains certain nanomaterials, you may need to provide more information.

How you do this depends on whether the nanomaterial is listed in Annex 4, 5 or 6 of EC regulation 1223/2009.

How to tell us what's in the cosmetic product

How you do this depends on whether or not the product was notified in the EU before Brexit.


Notified in the EU before Brexit

You can upload the data from your EU notification. You'll need to:

  • export your data as a ZIP file for each notification – the CPNP allows you to do this
  • upload the ZIP files - you can upload 100 files at a time

The ZIP files will not include:

  • the formulation of the product if it was an attached document – you must provide this separately as a PDF, RTF or TXT file
  • any images – you’ll need to provide them for products notified after Brexit
  • details of the nanomaterial
Not notified in the EU

You'll need to manually enter information about the product.
